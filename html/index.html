ANIMAL CONSERVATION


What is Wild Life Conservation

  • Wildlife conservation is the preservation and protection of animals, plants, and their habitats.
    By conserving wildlife, we're ensuring that future generations can enjoy our natural world and the incredible species that live within it.

How can Animals be Conserved

  • One of the easiest and most effective ways to help wildlife is to preserve the environment in which the animals live. Volunteer with organizations in your area to restore native forests,
    grasslands, and coastal ecosystems by planting native species, manually removing invasive plant species, and taking out old fences.

Why is conservation important for animal

  • As part of the world's ecosystems, wildlife provides balance and stability to nature's processes. The goal of wildlife conservation is to ensure the survival of these species, and
    to educate people on living sustainably with other species.

How do animals help the environment?

  1. Animals help plants reproduce by carrying pollen from plant-to-plant and by spreading seeds
  2. Animals that eat insects and small mammals help reduce damage by caterpillars, rabbits, and other plant-eaters.
  3. In temperate, tropical and subtropical grassland ecosystems, large animals can reduce forest and bush fires, increase albedo and help retain carbon in the vegetation and the soil

How do plant help the environment?

  • Clean the Air As part of the process of photosynthesis, plants absorb carbon dioxide and convert it to energy.
  • Bring More Rain Plants also help to regulate weather patterns, such as when and how often it rains.
  • Provide Resources During the process of photosynthesis, plants convert energy from the sun into sugar and other usable forms of energy.
